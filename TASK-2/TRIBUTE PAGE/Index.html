A Tribute to

VIRAT KOHLI

"Virat Kohli is a former captain and an Indian International cricketer who plays as a right-handed batsman for Royal Challengers Bangalore in the IPL and for Delhi in Indian Domestic Cricket"

Virat Kohli is referred to as the ‘King’. The Indian captain’s batting record is hardly matched by any other cricketer on the planet, with his aggressive approach to the game also making him a player fans flock to watch.

Kohli always dreamed of representing his country in cricket and had his first opportunity as a teenager when he played for the India under-19 team in 2006, guiding the same team to a World Cup win in 2008.

Virat Kohli’s brilliance with the bat is matched by his skills as a captain in all three forms of the game. While a major tournament win still eludes the Indian’s under his leadership, the side has regularly held the #1 world ranking in both Test and ODI cricket. He has the greatest Test Match winning percentage as an Indian captain, with a most recent triumph being a 2-1 series victory against Australia – the first ever Indian series win on Australian soil.

Most double centuries by an Indian in Test cricket

In 2019, Kohli surpassed Sachin Tendulkar and Virendra Sehwag to smash most double centuries by an Indian in test cricket. Kohli has seven centuries in the long format of the game, while Sachin and Sehwag have six double centuries each.

Most runs in a single IPL season – 973

Kohli scored 973 runs in the ninth edition of the Indian Premier League to guide his team to the finals of the tournament. The season saw him score as many as four centuries — the most by a player in a single season

Most runs in a calendar year/series

In 2017, Kohli slammed 11 centuries across all formats of the game and amassed 3000 runs. As a captain, he led India to new heights, winning all the series at home. Most ODI runs in a calendar year by an Indian cricketer – 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016

Virat Kohli’s ODI match records

As of January 2023, Kohli is the fifth-highest run-scorer (12809) in ODI matches. His batting average stands at 58.23. He has scored 46 ODI centuries and is only three centuries behind Sachin Tendulkar’s record of 49, has hit 1,204 fours and made 141 catches in ODIs.

Greatest Test Captain

Virat Kohli is indeed India's greatest Test captain. With 39 victories from 66 attempts, Kohli holds the record for the most wins as Indian Test skipper, surpassing his predecessor MS Dhoni, who captained the side to 27 victories.

Kohli also took India from 7th in the ICC Test rankings in 2014, all the way to the top of the rankings by the time he stepped down from the role in January 2022.

The 34-year-old also scored the most runs in Test cricket as captain of any Indian player in history with 5,864. The only players across the world stage who sit ahead of Kohli in this statistic are Graeme Smith, Allan Border and Ricky Ponting.
